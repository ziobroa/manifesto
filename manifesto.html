I design for a we.
I design for expression,
representation,
provacation.
I design not for following,
not for trends,
not for rules,
when those rules are meant for breaking.
I design not for the man
(or I try).
I design not to feed capitalism
(I do wish).
I design for education,
for accessibility,
for simplicity,
for complexity,
for an experience,
for an idea.
I design for a better us.
